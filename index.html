Preparation

Joumou soup is a traditional Haitian soup. It is part of Haiti's cultural heritage. Since December 16, 2021, it has been inscribed on the Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity for Haiti.

It's made with giraumons, a type of squash closely related to the pumpkin. Giraumon is said to be the local pumpkin of the West Indies. Slices of giraumon are simmered in a pot with beef, potatoes and vegetables (parsley, carrots, kale, celery and onions). Everything is blended and seasoned with garlic, herbs and spices. Vermicelli and a little butter and oil are also added. The soup is always served hot and sometimes accompanied by bread. *** Translated with www.DeepL.com/Translator (free version) ***
